

## Overview
**World War II** (1 September 1939 – 2 September 1945) was the largest and deadliest global conflict in history, fought between two major alliances:
- **Allies**: United States, United Kingdom, Soviet Union, China, France, and others
- **Axis Powers**: Germany, Italy, Japan

Over **60–75 million people** died, including millions of civilians due to genocide, bombing, famine, and disease.

---

## Causes
- Unresolved tensions after World War I
- Harsh conditions of the Treaty of Versailles
- Rise of **fascism** in Europe and **militarism** in Japan
- Expansionist policies of Nazi Germany, Fascist Italy, and Imperial Japan
- Failure of the League of Nations to prevent aggression

---

## Major Events (Timeline)
- **1931** – Japan invades Manchuria  
- **1937** – Second Sino-Japanese War begins  
- **1 Sept 1939** – Germany invades Poland → war begins in Europe  
- **1940** – Germany conquers much of Western Europe; Battle of Britain  
- **22 June 1941** – Germany invades the Soviet Union (Operation Barbarossa)  
- **7 Dec 1941** – Japan attacks Pearl Harbor → USA enters the war  
- **1942–1943** – Turning points: Midway (Pacific), Stalingrad (Eastern Front)  
- **6 June 1944** – D-Day invasion of Normandy  
- **April–May 1945** – Fall of Berlin; Germany surrenders  
- **6 & 9 Aug 1945** – Atomic bombs on Hiroshima and Nagasaki  
- **2 Sept 1945** – Japan formally surrenders

---

## Key Turning Points
- **Battle of Stalingrad (1942–43)** – Halted German advance in the East  
- **Battle of Midway (1942)** – Destroyed Japan’s naval superiority  
- **D-Day (1944)** – Opened Western Front against Germany

---

## End of the War
- **Germany surrendered**: 8 May 1945 (V-E Day)
- **Japan surrendered**: 15 August 1945 (formal signing on 2 September)

---

## Aftermath
- Creation of the **United Nations (1945)**
- Division of Germany and Europe → Cold War
- USA and USSR emerged as superpowers
- Decolonisation of Asia and Africa
- War crimes trials (Nuremberg and Tokyo)
- Massive reconstruction (Marshall Plan)

---

## Impact
- Holocaust: ~6 million Jews murdered
- Genocide, war crimes, and mass civilian deaths
- First and only use of **nuclear weapons** in war
- Major advances in military technology and medicine

---

## Summary
World War II reshaped global politics, economies, and societies, directly leading to the Cold War and shaping international relations for the rest of the 20th century.
